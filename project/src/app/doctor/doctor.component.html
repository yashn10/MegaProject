What Are Medicines?

Medicines are chemicals or compounds used to cure, halt, or prevent disease; ease symptoms; or help in the diagnosis of illnesses. Advances in medicines have enabled doctors to cure many diseases and save lives. Many medicines are developed from substances found in nature, while others are made in labs by mixing chemicals. Some, like penicillin, are byproducts of organisms such as fungus.
